deihätsō
adjective: being a collector; sentimental
noun: a collector or gatherer of materials
From Proto-Jästūgei dāhatso, gatherer, a title derived from de'o (orig. doi), go and hīatsē (orig. hīahtsē), edible plant.
deitäd
adjective: friendly
noun: a friend
From Proto-Jästūgei dātād, formed by affixing dæ, from dā, good, to the beginning of tꝏæd, person.
eit
adjective: having not had sexual intercourse
noun: a person who has not had sexual intercourse; a virgin
From Ancient Jästūgei æt, a simplification of Proto-Jästūgei sæd dāt, good female
Note: eit was originally considered offensive (emasculating) when used to describe men; this stigma has only recently faded
