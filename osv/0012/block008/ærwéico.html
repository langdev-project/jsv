ærwéico

Etymology:
From X ærwéilso, from VIV *ûxwô-deitsô «presented as though brought together». Originally used as a transitive verb with the sense «bring together»; this meaning was taken over in XI by the similar-sounding ærwéido (now ǽrodo). Sense «dense» is from XI.

intransitive verb

  1. (archaic) be whole; be complete
  2. be dense; be weighty
