lo

Etymology:
From VIV *-tau «nothing». Plural form a contraction of X lo åiz.

article

plural: loiz

generic article

Note:

lo is used to refer to a generic entity, akin to the English any. For the inanimate equivalent, see mæ.

The plural form loiz generally only appears in the object of a sentence, as the null article achieves the same effect in the subject.
